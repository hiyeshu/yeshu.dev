[Economics] Cost is the biggest price of giving up
Let's say you have a store.
The real estate company wants to buy this piece of land for development.
This is because developers can charge higher prices for commercial housing and bring greater value.
### Cost is the biggest price of giving up
In other words, the cost of your house is the demolition fee you gave up by not demolishing it.
A piece of land can not only be used to build a shopping mall or a residential complex, but it can also be used for many other purposes: farming, parks, basketball courts, etc. When a resource has many options, the cost of the selected option is the one with the highest value among all the options that were given up.
In other words: the cost of a sum of money is the price you get by **giving up** the maximum value of the money.
### Sunk costs are not costs
Sunk costs: Expenditures that have been incurred and cannot be recovered, such as money, time, energy, etc. that have been paid, are sunk costs.
Sunk costs are everywhere in our daily consumption, from the cashier asking "do you have a membership card" first when checking out at the supermarket, to recharging 200 yuan to get 50 yuan, the deposit in Taobao live broadcast, and even Pupu's buy-three-get-one-free benefit.
We are immersed in this kind of preferential benefits, but your sunk costs are being used by merchants.
Of course, it is impossible for people to be rational in everything, otherwise people would not be anxious, conflicted, sparkling and beautiful.
**Sunk cost itself is not good or bad. If the product is what you need,**
Just like what is written in "The Little Prince", "It is precisely because you spend time on roses that your roses become so important."
### Negative feelings are not a cost
In order to understand this concept, let's think about a question. For example, if you have a yard, you can choose to build a swimming pool or a barbecue station in the yard, but you can't have both, how should you choose? We provide a series of numbers. For example, the positive feeling of building a swimming pool is 100 points, but because of the fatigue, sweat and mess during the construction process, it brings you 30 points of negative feeling. Your net happiness of building the swimming pool is 70 points; the positive feeling of building a barbecue station is 80 points, the negative feeling is 40 points, and the net happiness of the barbecue station is 40 points.
So is the cost of building a swimming pool worth 30 points of negative feelings, and the cost of building a barbecue table is 40 points of negative feelings?
None! Cost is the biggest price of giving up. What you give up when building a swimming pool is the barbecue platform. What you give up when building a barbecue platform is the swimming pool. The swimming pool and the barbecue platform are costs of each other. You can't say that building a swimming pool brings you 30 points of negative feelings. The cost of building a swimming pool is 30 points. Negative feelings are not costs, the biggest price of giving up is the cost.
### The cost is determined by others
Suppose our family has an ancestral shop on the street, and its business is selling tea eggs. To this day, I still insist on selling tea eggs in my shop. My idea is that since this shop belongs to our family and we don’t have to pay rent, the cost for me to continue running the tea egg business is zero. Do you think this is a right idea?
This idea is wrong. Of course, this shop belongs to our family and the property rights are ours, so we don’t need to pay rent. However, we cannot forget the concept of cost in economics.
What are the costs? Cost is the biggest price of giving up. I insist on using this shop to sell tea eggs, and the cost is to give up other possibilities. If I rent this shop to others, I can make a lot of money, so I insist on selling tea eggs. What I give up is the rent I can earn by renting out this shop.
Who decides the rent? It is not decided by our family, but by all other people in society. It is their opinions that determine how much the rent of this location on Chang'an Street is worth, so it is other people in society that determine the cost of my insistence on selling tea eggs. Changes in their views will affect my cost of selling tea eggs.
If someone is willing to pay 20,000 yuan to rent this shop, then the cost of insisting on selling tea eggs is 20,000; if someone is willing to pay 30,000 yuan, then the cost of insisting on selling tea eggs is 30,000 yuan; if it is 40,000 yuan, the cost is 40,000 yuan. It has nothing to do with who owns the house. The cost of insisting on selling tea eggs has nothing to do with one thing, and that is the biggest income opportunity given up.
So your costs are determined by others
