## 💬 20 suggestions for “workers”
1. Select work priority: Interest > Experience accumulation opportunities (that contribute to your mid- to long-term personal goals) > Salary and benefits
2. Determine whether the company has development: track > waist of track > marginal positions
3. Authenticity > Self-drive/sense of mission/awareness of goals/ambition > Learning ability (information literacy has the highest proportion) > Professionalism (execution ability has the highest proportion) > Experience
4. Interesting work: Doing it without paying (love) > Others find it tiring, but you don’t feel it (comparison, superiority) > The ceiling of this job (the best so far)
5. The perfect job: you do it without any money + others find it tiring but you don’t feel it
6. Three long-term common compound interest skills: statistics, programming, and writing
7. Record work notes, because they cover writing, review, planning, and communication skills
8. Thick snow on long slopes and thin snow on long slopes: If you don’t have talent, try to choose an industry with “slow ability”, where there are few people and wide roads.
9. Prepare for the worst: If I am going to be fired at the end of this year, what should I do to improve my job-hopping success rate?
10. "Airport test" for selecting leaders/colleagues - If our flight is delayed, am I willing to wait at the airport with this person?
11. It is extremely useful for service output, extremely useful for leadership emotions, and extremely useful for maintaining the company. If one of these three "useful" skills is full, it is unlikely to be eliminated.
12. Have 3 universal stories ready that fit your resume. Like Lego bricks, they can be applied and combined into personal experience interview questions, such as "Tell me about a time when you encountered a difficult situation" and "Tell me about a cooperative relationship that impressed you deeply."
13. Quantify your personal value,
14. If you join a startup, don’t be afraid to ask for options when negotiating salary.
15. Don’t be path dependent and try to find a job you don’t like and transition first. Think clearly about the best combination point and get it right in one step.
16. Understand the profit margin of the company's main products and calculate whether it can cover the cost. In this economic environment, companies that are still burning money will most likely be struggling unless their "market valuation" is high enough.
17. Being too ugly, too beautiful, too inexpressive, and too expressive can all be problems.
18. Rent a house close to the company.
19. Don’t hang yourself from a tree in your ideal position, find a spare tire.
20. In negotiations, those who don’t care always win.
