## Productize yourself
> A hot search will attract most people because of its powerful eye-catching topic. Then after most people know about it, this hot search will be eliminated or replaced by other hot searches, returning to the popularity that this hot search should have.
> A new career will be created because
**Super high initial rate of return**
When making products, you must first have a product that is better and more reliable than competing products. After that, you should start to focus on marketing, public relations and other growth strategies.
The same goes for career development:
**Ensure retention before growth, so that you can create a deep and lasting impression on those who come into contact with you. **
The way products go wrong is surprisingly similar to career development issues:
- Do more functions instead of honing existing functions (building a network instead of focusing on reputation cultivation) (thinking about changing careers, but doing things that have nothing to do with the goal every day)
- Entering a sunset track (such as working in a sunset industry)
- Copying competing products and creating undifferentiated products (choose career direction to follow the trend)
- Choose a market that is too niche (such as an industry that is too vertical)
- Get input from non-key users (career advice from a relative)
- Too closed, not looking at what is happening in the outside world (working overtime every day, leaving no time to think) (stuck in one's ways, not learning often)
### Career Development
When making a career choice, the most important question to ask is: **What things can you do today that will make it easier for you to obtain the resources and opportunities you want tomorrow? **
Most of the time, people don't need you, but your skills - **When they come to you for dinner, they mostly turn to you for the professional knowledge behind you, not for lack of people to accompany them to dinner**.
A simple metric: whether these abilities can be trained. **
### Career Thoughts
- What is easy for you to do but difficult for others.
- What skills and assets are difficult for others to copy.
- Pick something to do that is small now, but will be big in the future.
- If you were required to invest 10,000 hours now, what would you choose?
It’s best to find the intersection of 2-3 things, even if you’re not good at any one of them.
