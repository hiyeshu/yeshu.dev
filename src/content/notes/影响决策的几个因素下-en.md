## Several factors affecting decision-making (Part 2)
In the last issue, we mentioned that "subjective intuition", "comparison object" and "emotion" affect decision-making.
The reason is all because of the brain’s lazy mechanism. This lazy mechanism is described as "System 1" in "Thinking Fast and Slow"
It is unconscious and fast, requires little mental effort, has no feelings, and is completely under voluntary control.
There is also a non-lazy "System 2" that is responsible for complex calculations, usually associated with subjective experiences such as behavior, choice, and concentration.
## Conformity phenomenon
"Everybody does it, so I figured I should do it too."
There are two reasons for this phenomenon:
1. **(Animal Instinct) Avoid thinking**, believing that in uncertain situations, the majority is correct, reducing the cost of thinking/obtaining answers.
2. **(Group recognition) Fear of being isolated,** in order to seek recognition from others or groups.
3. Avoiding responsibility (fear of loss) and believing that following the herd is the least likely solution to problems. Even if the "herd" I follow makes a mistake, I will make the same mistake together. **
Here we focus on solutions to 3.
