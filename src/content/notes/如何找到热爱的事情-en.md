## "Viewpoint" How to find what you love?
A simple truth is "**You must love something to do it well**", but it is really difficult to find what you love.
What is something you are passionate about?
First of all, there is no standard answer to this question. We can only guide ourselves to think through the way we ask questions.
Ask yourself four questions first:
### 1. If the incidental benefits of this matter were given directly to me, would I continue to do this?
(You said you love fitness. If you now have permanent eight-pack abs/slim figure, you can no longer exercise. Would you be willing?)
The problem is that the consequences of thinking about things affect the motivation for doing them.
### 2. If I can only do this thing silently and cannot tell others, will I continue to do it?
(You said you love traveling. If you were allowed to have unlimited free travel, but you couldn’t tell others, such as posting on Moments, etc., would you be willing?)
This question is to measure whether the driving force for doing something is external motivation, which may be the approval of others.
### 3. What I want to do now is A, and then list another thing B I want to do. I can only choose one. The other one needs to be given up and cannot be done again. Are you willing?
(After doing A, you can no longer do B)
The question is to ask yourself to think about how much you would give up for something.
### 4. If I only had ten years to live, would I still do this?
(We will overestimate what will happen in one year and underestimate what will happen in ten years. In many cases, we will default to one year as the largest unit to evaluate some things.)
Finally, maybe the question you want to ask is not "**How to find a job you love?**"
But **"I am not satisfied with my current work/life status. I hope that by finding a job I love, I can change the current situation and situation."**
