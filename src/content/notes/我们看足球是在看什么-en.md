> Football is a war in peacetime
### ⚽️ When we watch football, what are we watching?
In the 2022 Qatar World Cup, Japan won the double championship and lost to the runner-up. It took Japanese football thirty years to complete the counterattack. **
What is the reason for a team that was similar to the Chinese team thirty years ago to counterattack thirty years later?
#### 🥅 Behind Football
A formal professional football match requires at least 60 people including staff.
This means that each game requires a large number of professionals to be present.
The probability of selecting these professionals is 22 to the nth power. You can understand that it takes a huge population base that plays football to select people who can participate in professional football games (including players, referees, coaches, medical staff...)
The golden age of football players is between 18 and 27 years old. After the age of 27, their physical fitness will more or less decline, so a new generation must be replaced every 7-8 years.
This means that only by starting football training at the age of 6-7 can we have a steady stream of talent resources.  
But football is different from intellectual sports. Intellectual genius can be observed at a very young age, but football takes 12-13 years to play.
This is why Chinese intellectual sports (such as Go) can achieve good rankings, but football cannot.
Because football requires many people to start playing football at the age of 6-7 and practice for 12-13 years, and all age levels are reasonable, so that there will be continuous talents. **
#### 🇦🇷 Behind the Stars
Messi was diagnosed with growth hormone deficiency when he was a child, which means he cannot grow taller. It was Rexach who had a unique vision and signed Messi on a tissue.
In other words, a genius needs to be selected. **
However, doesn’t the person who selected him need to be selected? **

#### 🇯🇵 Japanese youth training
In a huge football system, who will be the youth training coach?
[Zhao Dayu](https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E8%B5%B5%E8%BE%BE%E8%A3%95/10995784?fr=aladdin) is a former Chinese national football player. Because he is only 1.6 meters tall, he is known as the "dwarf tiger". In 1984, he led the Chinese team to defeat the Argentine team. After retiring, he taught primary school youth football in Japan.
And only this kind of person can discover geniuses that people of lower level than him cannot.
#### 🇨🇳 China
China needs football talents and needs society to bear huge costs.
Economic development requires people, and football wastes a large part of human resources. (In fact, Japanese football only emerged after Japan’s economic slowdown)
But most people who criticize the Chinese team do not want their children to play football. After all, the probability of being successful in football is much harder than working a nine-to-five job.
Looking at domestic clubs, they don’t want players to stay abroad because they feel that it takes too much to train a player and they are afraid that the player will not come back.
However, foreign clubs such as Japan do not even ask for transfer fees, and they still want to play abroad.
> Before the 2022 World Cup, the number of Japanese players in Europe reached a record 451, including more than 200 players in Germany. Some people joke that Japan is the second Bundesliga team and Germany is Bayern.
The high salaries of domestic clubs are also the last straw for Chinese football.
Everyone on the field doesn't want to get injured, because if you are injured, you can't play as the main player, and if you can't play as the main player, you won't get high wages.
