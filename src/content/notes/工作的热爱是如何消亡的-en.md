## How does the love of work die?
- Why do most people stop loving their jobs after working for a while?
-Why do some people love their jobs and work nonstop throughout their lives, while others don't?
### 1. Go to work ≠ work
Most people work directly from their leaders, or accept them passively. So I rarely work seriously. Even if you work hard, it is mandatory or there is a high possibility of being rewarded with salary.
The "go to work" mentioned here means not thinking about whether the work assigned by the leader is really effective. Because of being criticized or being rewarded with salary, I just execute it without thinking.
Some people who are good at office politics know that they will not make any progress after working overtime, but they always make themselves into the "**office overtime scene**"**, so everyone thinks about working overtime and rarely do anything to **reduce the workload**. This confusion is part of the reason why people today think their jobs are meaningless.
Because in the workplace, assessment is based on the principle of superficial love for work.
This means that people who really want to be more productive and valuable at work and leave work early are at a disadvantage. It's as if blindly working overtime is to obtain a certificate. These certificates are not a reflection of the value of work, but how hard a person is willing to work to satisfy the office landscape.
But wasting your time isn’t the worst thing a jerk company can do to you. Worst of all, it teaches you that the way to win is to work "overtime."
### 2. Leaders** only talk**
Your leaders will tell you that you need to love your job, but they don’t love it themselves, or they only act like they love it.
Because leaders who really love their work will not talk boringly about overtime and how hard it is. Because for them, the results of work are greater than meaningless overtime.
They know that loving your job is not something the company can persuade you to do, but is a natural result of loving it. **
“Make people work hard” changes to “Make people love things.”
They should cultivate their followers' innate love and ability to question decisions.
What is ridiculous is that leaders are afraid of this kind of critical thinking and the ability to question decisions. **Because it will cause many leaders to lose their jobs and authority.
### 2. Knowing too little about work
Another way passion at work can sabotage is when your company makes you think there's only one way to get the job done.
The company's entire process is oversimplified, and you can only see a certain aspect of the process for which you are responsible.
This simplification is helpful for leaders to arrange and guide work, because questioning decisions is not conducive to leaders completing the work assigned by their superiors.
Good leaders guide our work while cultivating our enthusiasm by pointing out what the value of our work is across the entire company and how to embody or enhance that value.
There are two reasons why not many leaders do this.
- Most people leaders don’t know the limitations of their fields and knowledge
- The number of leaders will be very limited and they are afraid of losing their jobs if they are not replaced by their subordinates
### 3. Work cannot be made simpler
Due to the leader's incompetence or in order to establish authority, some bizarre and incomprehensible actions are usually arranged and directed.
As a result, seemingly simple and certain tasks become more subtle and uncertain.
But the truth is, the complexity of the job is not constantly increasing. After spending enough time and energy to understand the value of the work, things become simpler, because you can judge whether the leader's arrangement is really effective for the work. This is why the seemingly complicated work is actually very simple.
The simple reason most people never experience this is that they confuse "going to work" with "working"
Because "going to work" cannot filter out erroneous work information, it will only increase the complexity of work, and only a few people have experienced simple work.
### **4. The death of the possibility of work**
Job responsibilities take away all the fun from work and force everyone to work in a strict and planned manner.
Using the same job responsibilities to apply everyone's inevitable shortcomings.
There is only a limited space to work in, making the work content more difficult and boring as a result.
The solution is to find something you are passionate about.
We live in an age where anyone with the desire to learn and the Internet can learn almost anything for free.
Once you start learning to make things on your own—out of sheer curiosity—you'll notice the difference between understanding what works and just memorizing something.
Many job information in the past was for promotion and salary increase. Now you can decide which information is effective for job value and which is not.
This skill—detecting a signal in the noise of all the information and then discarding everything else—is one of the most important skills to master in our modern information age.
By allowing yourself to only learn things that naturally interest you, you can learn to distinguish between useful information and misleading information at work.
### **5. The role of the Internet**
The Internet helped solve this problem by providing a free market for work.
There is no longer a leader who decides what work is “useful” and what learning is “important.”
Most jobs currently in companies may not survive the test of the free market on the Internet (and the test of time), especially those that are more concerned with office politics than professionalism, will have a hard time surviving because they provide no value.
Overall, this is a positive view of the future of work. We have been able to learn anything we want on our own, with little or no money, on the Internet.
The only thing you need is a love for your job—think of studying as something for fun in your “free time.”
