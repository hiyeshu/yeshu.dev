## 💬The opposite of the "Viewpoint" goal is another goal
**Goals** are divided into two types.
- One is the tactical level, which has a simple, clear, and measurable execution plan, such as how many kilometers I want to run every day.
- One is the strategic level, which is macroscopic and uses various "tactics" and paths to achieve this goal. For example, I want to have eight-pack abs.
- As can be seen from the above, the formulation of strategic goals can be a good way to plan tactical goals.
### So how to set a **good goal**?
In fact, the core of the goal is **choices**, and the goal is to give and gain.
In other words, "the opposite of one goal should be another goal."
For example: Your strategic goal is to "become a self-disciplined person." The opposite of this goal is to "become a person who is completely unable to self-discipline." This is actually very absurd. And "becoming an early to bed and early to rise person" is a good goal, because it means that you give up going to bed late and staying in bed, and giving up checking Weibo very late at night - this goal may not be suitable for everyone, but it must be a good goal.
In life, many people make the mistake of "wanting everything". "Wanting everything" means no trade-offs, no focus, no tilting of resources, and no repeated polishing and calculation, so the final result is destined to be something that doesn't work well. It's important to set an accurate and ambitious goal for yourself.
Setting goals requires reverse thinking. **Instead of asking how to do something, it is better to ask how not to do it. **
