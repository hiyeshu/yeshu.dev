## 💬 [Success] Reasons affecting promotion
If you roughly classify success, it can be divided into "internal scoreboard" and "external consensus".
The internal scoreboard will not be explained here. What we often say, "earning one million a year, owning a car and a house" actually belongs to the external consensus, which is a consensus in society.
And promotion is naturally an external consensus.
## Social Network
If a small company is compared to a small society, then the social network means a relatively stable relationship system (not interpersonal relationships) formed during work in a company.
When a person's ability and performance can be quantified, such as a salesperson's sales performance, then naturally whoever sells more will be promoted.
Then when abilities and performance cannot be quantified and can only be judged qualitatively, then social networks will play a decisive role.
In other words, when the abilities of some promotion candidates cannot be distinguished in absolute terms, those who are better able to maintain a relatively stable relationship system at work will win.
To put it simply, people will be less jealous if this person is promoted than if someone else is promoted.
So it’s understandable that those people who work overtime until late at night when they don’t have much work to do, try to please their colleagues, work hard, and like to suck up to others, can be promoted.
Because when work ability cannot be distinguished in absolute terms, some of your other abilities can be recognized by most people in the company, and then you can win the promotion.
In many companies, work ability is a consensus that most people cannot agree with, because most people have no idea what real work ability is. Only working overtime and being able to flatter people are the consensus that most people agree with.
## Good at expressing
There is a Chinese pianist whose name is Dui Zi. Every time he plays the piano, his movements are very large. In fact, he did this for a purpose.
In a piano competition, it is extremely difficult to identify the playing level of a top pianist when none of the contestants make mistakes. The praise of his movements can easily form a memory for the judges and win the championship.
This is also the reason why those newly opened milk tea shops like to hire people to queue up, because customers naturally choose places with a lot of queues.
A successful craftsman is 50% craftsmanship + 50% self-promotion.
## How to do it?
- It is best if your ability is significantly different from others in quantitative terms
- Regardless of whether there is a quantitative difference, your ability also needs to be easily and cheaply disseminated (the dissemination here can be products, skills, including behaviors, etc.) so that you can gain praise from more people and be recognized by social networks. (Think of celebrities)
-Learn to express yourself very plainly

### Get promoted first?
So is it because I flattered my boss and got promoted and then I rose to prominence?
No, flattering can indeed lead to promotion at the beginning, but your ability can only circulate among the leadership, and there are so many flatterers that you cannot be recognized by the social network.
**If an ability cannot be quantitatively evaluated, then the most important thing is not the talent itself, but what people currently need. **
In other words, if you are very capable and your colleagues need a leader who will not let them work overtime, and you work overtime until late at night every day, your colleagues will not expect you to be the leader.
