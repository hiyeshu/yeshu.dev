"Results Management" was published in 1964 and has experienced nearly 60 years of baptism.
There is a paragraph at the beginning: "**Results are achieved by exploring opportunities, not by solving problems.**"
In other words, to achieve something, resources must be allocated to things that are more capable of achieving results.
**How to do things well is not the focus, the focus is "how to find the right thing to do and concentrate resources and strength to do it well"**
In fact, this is closely related to our lives. Observe the people around you. "Being exhausted" is a typical state. It seems that you have done a lot of things and are "constantly solving problems."
In fact, there are often only a few points that determine the result.
Therefore, we need to be brave enough to give up some things, discover our own strengths, and invest in things that look for opportunities rather than problems, in order to achieve meaningful results.
