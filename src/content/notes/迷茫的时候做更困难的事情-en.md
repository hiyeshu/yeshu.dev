## Do more difficult things
When we face a choice and need to decide what to do and how to do it, our instinctive reaction is to choose the simpler thing and path.
It is easy to eat a full meal, but difficult to diet and exercise.
Working out was always painful, so I was always on and off. Because when I finally finish a workout, I’m still in pain the next day.
But why did you persist this time? Not only is the amount of exercise reduced, the **psychological pain** will also be reduced with exercise.
>Exercise is a thing for me
**Make it simple**
**Go to exercise. **
Try thinking about it this way, **pain is not something terrible to be postponed and avoided, but a sign that you are becoming stronger** – something to savor and enjoy. This is what makes you better.
But many pains are really difficult to persist. How to solve them? The answer is not willpower, but **reducing the depth of touch and increasing the frequency of touch. **
The trick is not to escape. The whole world will become your testing ground and make you stronger.
So it's always good to start small, don't try to solve huge problems all at once, but sit down and admit that thinking about these things is painful, and then start to slowly break them down, and you will see that you are becoming more and more competent at this thing.
## Epiphany Addiction
> They feel energized every time they stumble upon some life-changing solution without pushing for any real-world change, and then when they return to real life, find things are the same as before, then continue to figure out how to fix them, and then continue to be excited by the new epiphany.
"Smart" people often fall into a trap, that is, they look down on small problems and always hope to make a splash on the "problem." Therefore, they do not take action immediately, but constantly "think and wait for opportunities." From an outside perspective, they seem to be strategizing and always waiting for something to come.
But from another perspective, it is actually a kind of escape. **
> When choosing a simple goal, you know you can always accomplish it so you don’t do it;
**When choosing a difficult goal, you will look for various ways to avoid failure (but you know that you will never fail if you don’t do it)**
If you can finally build 8-pack abs, can you start with a 20-second plank? Rather than pushing all your success into the indefinite future, see if you can pass a small test now.
**The important thing is to make these little things happen in the real world. **
