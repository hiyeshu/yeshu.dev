## **Why is "Sheep, a Sheep" so addictive?**
1) **Randomness. **Every time we reach the second level, we will look forward to the random arrangement of the second level. Or when shuffling the cards, it gives you the illusion that you are very close to passing the level, etc. These are all designs of randomness.
2) **Feedback is very fast. **It may only take a few seconds to consume each leafy green. Just like Douyin videos, videos of ten seconds slide down and then videos of ten seconds slide down.
3) **Someone can always pass. **When you enter the game, you are not on the game starting interface, but on the ranking list. This is intentionally designed to make you feel like someone is always getting through, in order to make you lucky enough to think you are one of them.
4) **Show off. **Whoever passes the level is the winner, and whoever is the darling of thousands of friends.
5) **Pursue certainty and escape from reality. **We usually imagine that we keep playing just to pass the level, but in fact there are very, very many people who clearly know that they are not doing it to pass the level, and they know that there is a high probability that they will not be able to pass the level.
Because they are looking for certainty. Use clicks to eliminate such mechanical actions, as well as the expected disappointment and the occasional excitement of passing the test. This cycle completely occupies your thoughts, thereby forgetting the more stressful or scary things in your life.
