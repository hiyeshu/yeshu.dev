Giving up or denying something reveals more information than description and approval.
## **How to obtain the absolute right to speak in communication? **
Thank you for watching!
Raymond mentioned in "Homesteading the Noosphere" that there are three modes of human beings gaining status.
-Imperative: status mainly comes from having the right to distribute certain scarce things
- Exchange: the mainstream model of our current society, voluntary cooperation, mutual help
- Gift style: Social status is not determined by what you control, but by what you give up - donations from multi-millionaires, etc.
### **The right to speak in communication**
- Imperative: This is what leader XX said
- Commutative formula: Both Little A and Little B have seen it and think it’s OK
- Gift style: Your plan is pretty good, just change it and it will be perfect.
**"Effective Speech"** also mentioned that the speech should start with "you" instead of "I", because everyone is very busy and only care about things related to their own interests. Tell them straight to the point what they need to know and why they need to know it.
For example: You worked hard today.
It is also a kind of "gift-like acquisition of the right to speak", an expression that gives up "I" and it is also very hard.
However, the requirements for "gifts" are also more stringent. This is because:
- The value of a gift depends on feedback from the recipient (i.e. how many people will know about or benefit from your gift). Therefore, the "gift given" is ineffective when it cannot be fully understood - for example, if no one has done anything, you just say it is hard work.
- The gift is not brand new - for example, someone has said it before, you say it again
- Gifts that are not widely accepted - For example, if you bear with me a little longer, I will finish it soon.
