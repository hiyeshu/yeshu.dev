The occurrence of behavior will be affected by motivation, ability, and prompts
## **How to change behavior? **
According to the "Fogg Behavior Model", the core formula for behavior change factors is:
> B (behavior) = M (motivation) A (ability) P (prompt)
> The occurrence of behavior will be affected by motivation, ability, and prompts
### **Motivation**
The motivations are complex. For example, if you see fat in your belly, you want to lose weight
Motivation is temporary and difficult to maintain. For example, if you want to lose weight, you buy a bunch of exercise equipment, and then you start to give up.
### **Ability**
Human beings are naturally not good at persisting in doing things that cause them pain in the long term. Instead, they mostly start with the easy ones.
Because there are many excuses, such as no time, no money, too tired today, this thing is too difficult, and there are other things to do...
Solution:
- Make the behavior small (don’t raise the bar too early and commit a larger behavior)
### **Prompt**
When people are both motivated and capable, they will respond reliably to prompts - for example, when playing on their mobile phones, they see the Weibo icon and browse for a while.
### **Example**
Why can Liu Genghong complete 40 minutes of exercises?
Motive: Everyone wants to lose weight
Ability: Simplify the movements so that everyone can follow them
Tips: Practice with Liu Genghong’s live broadcast and get constant reminders
