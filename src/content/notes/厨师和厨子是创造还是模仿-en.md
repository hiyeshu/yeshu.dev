### Chefs and cooks
The difference between "first principles thinking" and "analogical thinking", I refer to them as "chef" and "cook"
**"First Principles Thinking" (Chef)** is about thinking like a scientist - taking core facts and observations and drawing conclusions from their collision. Just like a chef experimenting with various ingredients to create a delicious meal. As long as you keep doing this, the chef will eventually create new recipes.
**And "analogical thinking" (cook)** means that you look at how things are already and then imitate them. The imitation process may include a personal touch, like a cook following a ready-made recipe.
A cook who follows a recipe exactly and a cook who invents a recipe completely independently are two extreme ends of the spectrum of thinking. In all the moments in your life when you need to think and make decisions, your thought process is always on this spectrum.
**Your choice is nothing more than whether you are more inclined to be a chef or a chef, to create or to imitate, to be original or to follow the trend. **
Being a chef requires a lot of time and energy. Because what you are doing is more difficult than reinventing the wheel. You are the first to invent the wheel. Being a chef is like groping in a misty forest while blindfolded. You will face many failures.
It's much easier to be a cook.
Most of the time, being a chef is a waste of time, and the opportunity cost is very high. After all, time is the most precious thing. I now wear branded clothes and branded sneakers. This is a way for me to follow the crowd when it comes to dressing. I often see people wearing the same brand of clothes as me when I walk on the road.
It is understandable that I choose to follow the crowd when it comes to dressing, because what I wear is not important to me. Clothes are not a way for me to express my personal characteristics. So for me personally, when it comes to fashion, I am willing to be lazy by thinking like a cook.
But in other parts of life, there are some things that are very, very important, such as where to live, who to make friends with, whether to marry, who to marry, whether to have children, how to raise children, and how to set priorities in life.
