You care so much about criticism because you accept praise.
## **What is praise? **
### **Praise is a means of control**
In the previous [The impact of language on behavior](https://yeshu.zhubai.love/posts/2175920066338574336), [A destructive tool](https://yeshu.zhubai.love/posts/2167868167240859648), we know that "**Language is a tool that can influence behavior**."
The same is true for the information conveyed through words such as praise. When you were a child, your parents praised you in the hope that you would behave as your parents wanted you to do, learn to walk, sleep alone, and eat all your food. These are nothing special things. After being praised by your parents, you have positive emotions and affect your behavior.
You can recall the behaviors you were praised for in your childhood. Are you doing better and better?
Through this kind of training, we learned that it is important to be praised and liked by others, because we can get food and care (this involves primitive tribal genes). When you enter society, you will find that you look forward to praise from your leaders, colleagues, and even your spouse.
But in the modern adult world, sincere praise is rare. Most of the time, praise from others is a soft rope.
Parents praise you for your growth, while some people just do it to save themselves worry, effort, and trouble. In essence, they are seeking personal gain.
### **Praise mostly comes from misunderstandings**
When I was young, I would go to my classmates’ houses to play and have dinner with them. The only vegetables are carrots, but I don't like eating them.
Because of etiquette issues, I insisted. Then his mother said, "See if people are more obedient."
It was then that I realized that the way many things appear is completely different from the praise given by others.
## **What is a celebration? **
### **Celebration is self-praise**
One way to create positive emotions is to celebrate our small victories. When we celebrate effectively, the reward pathways in the brain are activated.

By creating good feelings for yourself at the right moment, you prompt your brain to recognize and remember the behavior you just performed.

In other words, you can rely on celebration and self-reinforcement to “trick” your brain into forming a habit.
Say it to yourself: Awesome, it seems too artificial and of little significance.

But if you believe you are "awesome," then saying "awesome" to yourself is different.

Think about the victory screen after passing the game. It is essentially the game saying to you a seemingly insignificant "you are awesome". The pleasure this part of the game experience brings to players is experienced by everyone.
### **The role of celebration**
- Produce positive emotions: When you accomplish something (even by constantly lowering your standards) that you thought you couldn't accomplish, your brain will feel that it exceeded expectations and feel pleasantly surprised.
- Develop good habits: When we find that something is not as difficult as we thought, we slowly begin to like it
### **When to celebrate**
1. The moment you remember to implement a new habit
2. Moments when you are implementing a new habit
3. The moment you just complete a new habit
