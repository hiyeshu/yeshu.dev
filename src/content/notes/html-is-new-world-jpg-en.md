When a friend sends you an image, you do not ask for the source file.

That is how JPG is used. **Irreversible, meant to be looked at.** No layers, no structure, no meaningful way to keep editing.

HTML is becoming like this too.

It was not born that way. HTML was originally an intermediate state: interactive, structured, and processable by programs. It was born to be read, changed, and rendered again by another program.

But the moment it is generated by AI and handed directly to a person, its nature changes.

Because an HTML document is much easier to read. It can use tabs, images, and links to organize structure for browsing. It can even become responsive, giving you a suitable reading experience across devices.

**On the consumption side, HTML has already degraded into an image.**

## Programmability Is Wasteful for People

In the past, when we asked a model to write HTML, we were asking it to write "code." We looked at readability, structure, and whether it could be maintained. Back then, the value of HTML was its **programmability**.

The meaning of AI-generated HTML is no longer "code quality." It is "image quality" and "information density."

## Ted Chiang Stood on the Side of Markdown

In 2023, Ted Chiang wrote an essay saying ChatGPT is a blurry image of all the text on the web.

His logic was this: the model compresses the whole web into a JPG and loses truth. **Hallucination is loss.** The blur looks like understanding, but it is really repeating things with different words. So do not treat it as a search engine, do not use its generated content to fill the internet, and do not treat its first draft as the starting point.

In 2023, this was right. He stood on the side of truth.

## People Can No Longer Catch Markdown

Give you 1GB of raw Wikipedia text and you cannot finish reading it.

Even a Markdown file longer than 100 lines can already exhaust your patience.

People are not receivers with infinite bandwidth. **There is an upper limit to what a person can fit into their mind.**

When a model compresses that 1GB into three hundred words, it has to interpolate, merge, and drop words. The result is necessarily not strictly equal to the original.

This is what Ted Chiang calls hallucination.

It is also a form of compression.

MP3 works the same way.

It does not make sound completely smaller. It uses the human ear as a filter and removes the parts that people are unlikely to hear, or that do not affect understanding very much even if heard.

So MP3 is not sound itself. It is sound after being filtered through human perception.

JPG compresses the image the eye can catch.

MP3 compresses the sound the ear can catch.

The model compresses the text a person can catch.

HTML compresses the structure a person can catch.

They are not source files, but they are better suited for human consumption.

If you want people to **catch** something, you have to compress it. Compression will blur it. Blur will inevitably contain some hallucination.

**Hallucination is not a bug.**

"HTML is JPG" and "the model is JPG" are actually the same thing.

The model's text for people is one kind of JPG.<br>The model's interface for people, HTML, is another kind of JPG.

When the model reaches a point where a person can no longer discover hallucination, the ability to feel hallucination may be the biggest difference between humans and AI.
