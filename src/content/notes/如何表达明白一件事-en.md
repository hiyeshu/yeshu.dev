Only by expressing it can we truly understand
## **How to express that you understand something? **
When you want to express something clearly, there are generally two situations:
-Answer other people’s questions
- explain something to someone
### **When answering other people’s questions, question the question first**
Because a good question can get a good answer.
1) When answering someone else's "Why?", first ask "Is it true?" For example: "Why are you always bored?"
2) When a question involves a noun with unclear meaning, ask what the specific meaning of the word is? For example: “What does design research do?”
3) Does it make sense to answer this question? For example: "Which came first, the chicken or the egg?"
### **When explaining something to others**
**1) Show what you care about**
I have said it before [starting with "you"](https://yeshu.zhubai.love/posts/2178398270563766272), but its function is only to attract others' attention.
When you want to get specific, tell them what makes you think they care, not why they need to know. **
This thing is concrete, not feeling.
**2) Show first, tell later**
Be familiar first, then be unfamiliar, then compare.
**3) When speaking, use "therefore" and "but" instead of "then"**
**Bad story:** Someone got superpowers by accident, and then caused another person to die tragically, and then he learned a lesson from it, and then learned the responsibility of using superpowers from the lesson.
**Take Spider-Man as an example:** Someone was bitten by a spider and thus gained superpowers, but his irresponsibility led to the death of his uncle, so he learned a lesson, but he learned the responsibility of using superpowers in the lesson.
**4) Delete 10% of the words**
**Original word count:** Someone was bitten by a spider and gained superpowers, but his irresponsibility led to the death of his uncle, so he learned a lesson, but he learned the responsibility of using superpowers in the lesson.
**Deleted 10%:** Someone was bitten by a spider and gained superpowers, but his irresponsibility led to the death of his uncle, but he learned the responsibility of using superpowers in this lesson.
**5) Real test as early as possible**
Even if you think you've explained it clearly, you need to test it ahead of time to see if it actually works. Because after talking about other people’s feedback, it is often a feeling.
