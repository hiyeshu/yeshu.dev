## **How ​​have designer tools evolved? **
### **1️⃣ Timeline**
**1990**
- Photoshop: With pixels as the core, whether you are editing a photo, making a poster, drawing an illustration, or designing a website, you can do it all in PS.
- Illustrator: With vectors as the core, typography and some vector graphics drawing. However, the reusability is poor. For example, if you want to arrange a magazine with many pages, if you want to change one position, you have to change everything again.
**2010**
- Sketch: Focusing on UI design and projects, it has weaker functions than Illustrator, but many functions are tailor-made for UI designers. For example, it can reuse a grouped component many times, optimizes conventional UI design (rounded corners for button types), and has a wealth of plug-ins.
**2016**
- Figma: Based on Sketch, cloud collaboration is the core, with rich plug-ins and communities. More optimizations for UI and UX designers.
- Canva: With the purpose of the non-designer user base at the core, paired with plugins, templates and community. With "design purpose" as the core, a large number of templates and simple operations are used to meet the needs.
### **2️⃣ Reasons for evolution**
The Internet has given rise to entirely new uses for design. Photoshop is powerful, but many of these new uses aren't about image manipulation. Processing images is an integral part of the work that users want to complete. For example, a banner may need to be typeset, reused, modified in size, revised repeatedly, connected with upstream and downstream, and stored in files, etc.
**Changes in Design Content**
From TV commercials, leaflets and printed materials to public account first pictures and Xiaohongshu covers. From offline to online, there are more quantities, more iteration (one material can be changed at any time), file management and storage, version updates, and more material resources are needed.
**Changes in Design Process**
There are more intermediate links including operations, programmers, etc. that require upstream and downstream collaboration. Works cannot be delivered directly and collaboration is required.
**The size or dynamics of the market**
- Some of them were very good designs in the past (Thunder), but because the speed of the Internet has become less important, the technical environment of the Internet is changing very quickly.
- The market needs new things. No one will invest in Adobe anymore, but there will always be people who invest in companies trying to surpass Adobe.
### **3️⃣ Differences**
**PS has no official plug-in market and community**
- Canva creates marketplaces and communities with templates, layouts, fonts
- Figma creates markets and communities through plug-ins and resources
- Sketch has an official plug-in market, but the compatibility is poor
- Figma has a plug-in market and will be updated automatically
### **4️⃣ What can be learned**
- Break down the requirements to the smallest detail, so detailed that they can be connected with other tools
- Avoid fussing over details as they will become less important as demand changes
- There is no need to surrender to the big and all-powerful one. Even if Photoshop is all-powerful, it still has its opponents.
- Don’t underestimate the changes in the environment and continue to iterate with the environment
