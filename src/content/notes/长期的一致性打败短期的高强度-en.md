A few years ago, I watched the documentary ["The Other Me in the World"](https://movie.douban.com/subject/26855759/). In the European season, a young Danish lawyer said something he liked very much: Bruce Lee:
> Long term consistent can beat short term intensity.
> "Long-term consistency defeats short-term intensity"
Every time he travels to a place, he must learn to cook an authentic local dish. He said: I have been learning cooking since I was 13 years old. I am now 25 years old. Many people say that I am a great chef, but in fact I have spent 12 years on this. This is not something that can be done in a month or two. **I believe everything in life is like this. The longer you work, the more you gain. **

## Lighting
I went to a bookstore on the weekend and saw a child standing on the bookshelf reading comic books. His mother was standing beside him and reading with him. When the child saw the exciting plot, he would explain it to his mother, and his mother would respond patiently.
Until we finished visiting the bookstore and were about to leave, they were still reading comics there.
> The best way to build trust is to spend time.
Perhaps our relationship with our parents depends on how long we stand and respond patiently.
