## **Subjective intuition**
First of all, the brain is used to being lazy, because humans are used to keeping the brain in a low-load state during hunting in order to cope with the danger of wild beasts at any time. Because of this, when people receive some information that allows them to make instant decisions, they will subconsciously make a judgment immediately without carefully verifying the error.
For example: when I saw a program broadcasting a case of a perverted murderer, and a relatively gloomy picture of a child appeared, I subconsciously judged that "this child looks like a perverted murderer at first glance." However, the festival showed that this was the victim, and I subconsciously felt that he didn't look like a perverted murderer.
(Since he is a pervert - he most likely had a bad childhood experience - if he sees photos of dark children - he must be a perverted murderer)
### **So how to prevent subjective intuition from affecting decision-making? **
**When someone says something, don’t think about whether it is true, but think about the circumstances under which it would be true. **
The purpose of this is to prevent your own subjectivity and intuition from affecting your judgment (clothing, style, conversation...)
## **Comparison object**
**When the comparison object changes, the judgment will also change. **
For example, if I ask you "coffee, tea, hot chocolate", which one do you drink?
and respectively ask you "Tea or coffee?", "Hot chocolate or coffee?", "Tea or hot chocolate?"
There will be different options.
In the first question you will use (taste) for comparison, and in the second question you will choose (dense) (sweet and bitter) (calories) respectively.
The reason for this is that it is relatively simple and can produce results faster. **
Yes, the brain can still be lazy.
### **So how to prevent comparison objects from affecting decision-making? **
In fact, when we compare coffee, tea, and hot chocolate, we make judgments based on our own classification of drinks.
The more we classify this drink, the more criteria we have for judging it, such as (price) (degree of thirst quenching) (hot or cold) (quality), etc..
In this way, when the second question comes up, you will choose a category that can be compared, such as (price)
This is why we need to learn multi-disciplinary knowledge for the college entrance examination. The role of multidisciplinary knowledge is to enable you to learn better classification.
The "ge" of "investigating things to achieve knowledge" means how to classify objects.
Having multi-disciplinary knowledge will not allow you to directly select the most suitable one, but it can relatively prevent you from making bad decisions due to different comparison objects.
## **Emotion**
The prefrontal cortex of our brain is associated with executive brain functions, including planning, evaluation, problem solving, and determining the consequences of actions**, as well as processing emotions. **
In other words, if your mood fluctuates greatly, your prefrontal cortex will be running in both directions when making decisions.
Therefore, avoid making major decisions when you are in emotion.
### **How ​​to judge whether your emotions will affect your decision-making? **
A person will make two different decisions in the same situation. In fact, the brain is being interfered by his own emotions.
### **So how to prevent emotions from affecting decision-making? **
Sorry, 99% of people in the world are affected by emotions.
Useful method: Keep an emotional diary and record your decisions every time your mood fluctuates. Compare your past judgments next time.
