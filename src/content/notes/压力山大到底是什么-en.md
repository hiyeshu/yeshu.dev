## "Viewpoint" What exactly is stress?
- Stress is because in primitive societies, humans have a protective mechanism when they feel there are wild beasts or other dangers, which will put certain parts of your body into high-speed operation. Both humans and animals have the ability to foresee dangers, and they will also produce stress because of these predictions.
- **Glucocorticoids are the main culprit in stress**
- Stress can also cause some organs to stop working, such as the digestive system, etc.
- Modern humans cannot control where glucocorticoids go. They usually default to escape parts, such as feet and hands.
- But the biggest difference between modern humans and primitive societies is that the bad things we feel now will happen months, years, or even decades later
- This is the reason why this evolution cannot keep up with changes in the environment. Our stress has always been in a chronically activated state.
- This is where the problem starts.
**Chronic Stress in Humans**
- Chronic stress can cause your digestive system to shut down for more than a few minutes
-Chronic stress suppresses the immune system, making it harder to fight off disease, and is bad for your heart
- Stress doesn’t actually make you sick, but it can make you more susceptible to illnesses, which can be serious.
**How to deal with stress**
- Try to stay optimistic. Many things are actually very small if you think about them in years;
- **Enhance your sense of control, such as organizing your desktop, etc.**;
- Find a way to vent your emotions that suits you without hurting others, such as shouting;
- Communicate and share or complain
