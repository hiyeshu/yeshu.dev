## **Rest and Recovery**
Like happiness, we haven’t really learned how to rest. In today's consumer society, everyone's definition of rest can easily become the consumption of material or spiritual content, such as going shopping, watching dramas, playing games, etc. But in fact, these are not rest, but just recovery.
For example, after the National Day, I worked for seven consecutive days and felt very tired, so I naturally wanted to "relax" myself. As a result, I finished playing games secretly for a few hours and watched some documentaries. This not only did not make me feel more relaxed, but actually made me feel guilty. At this time, I want to study and read some books to make up for it, but it makes me even more tired.
### **What is recovery and rest? **
**▎Restore**
It means that you are so tired that you can only do some things for yourself, such as staying in bed, watching TV shows, or playing games, and you have no motivation to do anything else except simple, stimulating or numbing things. And these things all have a characteristic, that is, they can easily make you "addicted", constantly stimulating your brain to react.
This situation is very common, but it can only be called recovery. Although we also need such entertainment time, it does not allow you to completely recover.
**▎Break**
It means that you can have enough energy and motivation to do things on your own initiative, such as going out for a walk, cooking something, cleaning the house, etc. The core is that you should let yourself follow your feelings on this day and not do anything you have to do. There are no must-see dramas, no impromptu gatherings, etc.
**Rest is about feeling present and protecting yourself from the past (obligations) and the future (anxiety). **
In fact, rest can allow us to truly recover, but many people don’t know how to rest.
Instead, they choose more behaviors like those described in recovery, keeping their brains in "work" mode. If you continue like this for a long time, it will actually seem like you have spent many "weekends or holidays", but in fact you have not gotten any real rest, which will lead to burnout in the long run.
-Things that really bring about rest are things that free yourself from so many obligations and purposes, such as going for a walk in the park, taking a nap, listening to music, cleaning up the house, and cooking a delicious meal.
- Try to avoid anything that makes you addicted, such as reading instant Weibo, watching dramas, playing games, etc.
From this perspective, I feel tired. On the one hand, it is because many methods are just to recover, not to rest. The brain is still running, such as the plot trend in TV series, or the victory or defeat of the game;
If you're feeling tired, try this distinction to see if you can rest better.
